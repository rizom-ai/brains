The problem

Talent isn't the bottleneck. Coordination is.

Teams don't fail because people are untalented. They fail because nobody has mapped who knows what, who decides what, and how information moves. When you add AI into that, it doesn't help — it just automates the confusion.

The workshop

One session. A map your whole team can act on.

Outcomes-first: we map your team's transactive memory system — who knows what, how decisions flow, where information drops on the floor — and hand you a working diagnosis you can act on the next day.

01 Survey

A short async survey.

A questionnaire maps your team's current coordination patterns. We come prepared, so the workshop starts where the data leaves off.

02 Workshop

A facilitated half-day in the room.

Your team builds a shared map of expertise, decision authority, and information flow. Everybody sees the same picture — often for the first time ever.

03 Playbook

A diagnostic report and a thirty-day playbook.

Concrete changes to roles, rituals, and tooling — including how AI fits in without making the existing confusion worse.

The methodology

Thirty years of research. One consistent finding.

01Proven across industries

TMS is one of the most replicated findings in organizational psychology — validated across tech, healthcare, finance, and creative industries.

02Performance, not theory

Teams with strong TMS consistently outperform — across creativity, technical innovation, budget adherence, and problem-solving speed.

03The 70% benchmark

High-performing teams score above 70% on TMS measures. Most teams start well below that. The gap is always structural, not personal.

04Built for the field

Developed by Jan Hein Hoogstad through years of academic work and hands-on organizational practice.
